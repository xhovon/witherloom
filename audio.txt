Audio isn’t optional for your game—it’s one of the **main horror drivers**, especially since your visuals are procedurally generated. We’ll design it the same way: **fully code-driven, modular, and event-based**, so it plugs into your engine cleanly.

Below is a **complete audio system blueprint** that fits your architecture and emergent storytelling.

---

# 🔊 AUDIO SYSTEM DESIGN (ENGINE-COMPATIBLE)

## 🎯 Core Philosophy

* No static background music loops only
* Audio reacts to:

  * **Blight level**
  * **Time (day/night)**
  * **Player role & belief**
  * **Nearby events (NPC, danger, memory anomalies)**

👉 Sound = Information + Fear

---

# 🧩 AUDIO MODULE (FUTURE FILE)

Will become:

```
modules/audio_system.py
```

It will:

* Inherit from `BaseComponent`
* Subscribe to events (`events.py`)
* React dynamically

---

# 🔊 AUDIO LAYERS (CRITICAL DESIGN)

## 1. Ambient Layer (Continuous)

### Purpose

Sets mood of area

### Types

* Village calm (day)
* Night ambience (wind, insects)
* Forest (dense, whisper-like wind)
* Ruins (low rumble, distant echoes)
* Blight zones (distorted hum)

### Dynamic Behavior

* Volume & pitch change with:

  * time
  * weather
  * blight_level

---

## 2. Event Layer (Triggered)

### Examples

* Footsteps
* Door creak
* Item pickup
* Combat hit
* NPC scream
* Sudden silence (important)

---

## 3. Psychological Layer (Horror)

This is where your game becomes unique.

### Triggered by:

* Low sanity
* High blight exposure
* Being alone too long
* Night

### Effects

* Whispering (randomized)
* Reverse audio
* Faint calling of player name
* Footsteps behind player (no one there)

---

## 4. Music Layer (Rare, Strategic)

Use VERY sparingly.

### When used:

* Major story reveal
* Entering underground ruins
* Final chapter

### Style

* Minimal piano
* Drones
* Dissonant strings

---

# 🧠 AUDIO + GAME SYSTEM INTEGRATION

## Event Bus Usage (VERY IMPORTANT)

Audio listens to events like:

```
"player_moved"
"npc_screamed"
"blight_changed"
"night_started"
"item_used"
"sanity_low"
```

Audio system reacts:

* Plays sound
* Adjusts ambience
* Injects horror cues

---

# 🌑 BLIGHT-DRIVEN AUDIO

Each tile has `blight_level`

### Audio Behavior

| Blight Level | Audio Effect                    |
| ------------ | ------------------------------- |
| 0–20         | Normal ambience                 |
| 20–50        | Subtle distortion               |
| 50–80        | Whispers, low hum               |
| 80–100       | Audio glitches, reversed sounds |

# 🧍 CHARACTER-BASED AUDIO

## Role-Based Sounds

### Farmer

* Soil digging
* Crop rustle

### Doctor

* Glass, tools, heartbeat-like tones

### Guard

* Armor movement
* Weapon sounds

### Thief

* Soft footsteps
* Cloth movement

### Vampire

* Heartbeat of others louder
* Blood pulse sound

### Ghost

* Echo reverb on all sounds
* No footsteps

### Fox

* Animal hearing (amplified environment)

# 🧠 BELIEF-BASED AUDIO FILTERS

These subtly alter perception:

* Old Ways → nature whispers clearer
* Science → no distortion (until late game)
* Sun God → faint choir tones
* Moon Goddess → echo + delay
* Fatalist → muted, distant sounds
* Ancestor → voices of past layered

Even in 2D:

* Left/right panning
* Volume based on distance

* NPC scream far → faint
* Behind player → directional cue

# 📦 AUDIO GENERATION (NO ASSETS APPROACH)

Since you don’t have assets:

## Option 1: Procedural Sound (BEST FIT)

Generate sounds using:

* sine waves
* noise
* filters

Examples:

* Wind = filtered noise
* Blight = low-frequency oscillation
* Footsteps = short bursts

---

## Option 2: Minimal Embedded Samples

Small generated buffers:

* click
* thump
* noise burst

---

## Option 3: Hybrid (recommended later)

* Procedural base
* Add small sample pack later
