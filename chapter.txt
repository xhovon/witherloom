🕯️ WITHERBLOOM VALE — FULL CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE
GLOBAL STRUCTURE
Prologue + 8 Chapters + Endgame
Each chapter = 1 in-game week (fits your “World Tick” system)
Each chapter contains:
Core Event (always happens)
Role Variations
Belief Variations
Horror Escalation Layer
Oracle Events (randomized)
🌑 PROLOGUE — “THE ROAD THAT REMEMBERS”
Core Event

You arrive at Witherbloom Vale.

The road loops once.
You pass the same tree twice.
A villager greets you… then appears ahead of you again.
Key Mechanics Introduced
Stamina / Health
Dialogue tone (belief-based)
First relationship
Role Variations
Farmer: Recognizes land that “should not be fertile”
Doctor: Sees a corpse with no decay
Postman: Carries a letter already opened… by you
Thief: Notices someone following them since before arrival
Guard: Village defenses are strangely misaligned
Homeless: Denied entry to multiple homes
Magician Apprentice: Sees faint distortions in light
Vampire: Feels drawn to one specific house
Ghost: Remembers dying here (fragment only)
Fox: Smells something dead beneath the soil
Belief Variations
Science → “Environmental anomaly”
Old Ways → “Land spirit disturbed”
Religious → “Test or punishment”
Fatalist → “This already happened”
🌘 CHAPTER 1 — “THE FIRST ROT”
Core Event

Crops fail. Animals act erratically. A villager disappears.

Gameplay Focus
Basic survival
NPC routines
First investigation
Role Branches
Farmer: Crops grow overnight, then rot instantly
Herbalist: Finds plants that mimic medicine but poison slowly
Doctor: First patient shows “memory fever”
Guard: Night patrol hears footsteps in empty fields
Newspaperman: Writes first Blight report
Thief: Finds hidden food hoards
Patient: Other patients vanish from clinic
Horror Layer
Livestock born wrong (extra limbs, no eyes)
Wells taste metallic
Children talk to “someone underground”
🌒 CHAPTER 2 — “THE SECOND SELF”
Core Event

Multiple villagers report seeing duplicates.

Gameplay Focus
Social suspicion system
Trust / paranoia mechanics
Role Branches
Postman: Delivers letters written by “duplicates”
Guard: Ordered to arrest someone who is “in two places”
Doctor: Cannot medically distinguish real vs duplicate
Thief: Uses duplicates to frame others
Artisan: Crafts begin changing after completion
Ghost: Sees BOTH versions clearly
Vampire: Blood tastes different between copies
Belief Variations
Science → cloning / infection theory
Old Ways → “spirit reflection”
Moon → duality is natural
Horror Layer
One duplicate always disappears at night
The other begins behaving incorrectly
🌓 CHAPTER 3 — “THE NIGHT WITHOUT END”
Core Event

One night lasts too long.

Gameplay Focus
Night danger system
Fear / sanity pressure
Role Branches
Guard: Patrol never ends
Farmer: Crops grow ONLY at night now
Postman: No morning deliveries arrive
Homeless: Night creatures ignore you… or follow you
Magician: Time distortion becomes measurable
Fox: Night is safer than day
Horror Layer
Clocks stop
Lamps refuse to light
Some NPCs “freeze” mid-action
🌔 CHAPTER 4 — “THE BURIED VILLAGE”
Core Event

A sinkhole opens → reveals ruins below

Gameplay Focus
Exploration (dungeons)
Lore discovery
Role Branches
Miner: Finds structured tunnels
Doctor: Old mass grave
Herbalist: Ancient roots spreading upward
Thief: Hidden vaults
Ghost: FULL MEMORY FLASH (partial truth)
Chronicler’s Kin: Journal reacts for first time
Major Reveal

The vale existed before—and was destroyed.

Horror Layer
Skeletons arranged in rituals
Names match current villagers
🌕 CHAPTER 5 — “THE HOLLOW ROOT”
Core Event

You discover the Blight source: a living root network

Gameplay Focus
Choice-driven quests
Alignment begins
Role Branches
Farmer: Root is beneath all farmland
Doctor: Root behaves like a brain
Magician: Root reacts to will
Vampire: Root “speaks” directly
Ghost: Root recognizes you
Horror Layer
Roots move slightly
Whispering grows louder
NPCs begin worshipping or denying it
🌖 CHAPTER 6 — “THE FRACTURE”
Core Event

Village splits into factions

Factions
Burn the Vale
Study the Blight
Worship it
Ignore it
Gameplay Focus
Politics
Alliances
Betrayal
Role Impact
Guard: Enforces law → or becomes weapon
Newspaperman: Shapes public opinion
Shopkeeper’s Child: Controls supply chains
Outcast: Gains power in chaos
Thief: Profits from division
Horror Layer
Public executions
Riots
Secret cult formation
🌗 CHAPTER 7 — “THE MEMORY HARVEST”
Core Event

People begin losing identity

Gameplay Focus
Identity system
Memory-based mechanics
Effects
NPCs forget relationships
Names disappear from records
Player choices get rewritten
Role Branches
Doctor: Tracks “identity decay”
Postman: Letters addressed to no one
Ghost: Gains strength as others fade
Vampire: Feeding restores memory… temporarily
Horror Layer
You forget your own backstory options
Dialogue choices change mid-conversation
🌘 CHAPTER 8 — “THE ROOT AWAKENS”
Core Event

The Hollow Root becomes active

Gameplay Focus
Final preparation
Endgame path locking
World Changes
Terrain shifts
Buildings distort
Dead return (not zombies—echoes)
Role Climax Moments
Farmer: Must destroy or protect land
Doctor: Can attempt cure
Guard: Final stand
Thief: Escape route discovery
Ghost: Full truth revealed (you tried to stop this before)
Vampire: Choice to merge with Root
🔥 ENDGAME — “WHAT REMAINS”
Ending Paths (depend on ALL systems)
1. 🔥 Burn the Vale
Entire map destroyed
Survivors minimal
Blight contained… maybe
2. 🌑 Seal the Root
Cycle continues
Future generations doomed
3. 🧪 Cure the Blight (Hardest)
Requires:
Doctor/science path
Ritual knowledge
High relationships
Village survives, but changed forever
4. 👁️ Become the Keeper
Player becomes part of system
Gains control → loses identity
5. 🌍 Let it Spread (Bad Ending)
Vale becomes origin of global horror
